Project Overview

About AquaVision AI

A final-year, placement-ready research project combining classical deep learning with quantum computing for marine species identification — solving real-world underwater imaging challenges.

🔬 Research Problem

Underwater imaging suffers from light absorption (reds vanish below 5m), turbidity, backscatter, color shift, and blur. Standard CNNs struggle with these artifacts, producing false positives at rates up to 18%. Our hybrid quantum-classical approach reduces this to 2.6%.

📊 Dataset

Trained on 3.4M images from NOAA FishBase, the FishNet dataset, Kaggle Underwater Wildlife, and custom deep-sea footage. 120 species classes, balanced via synthetic augmentation (rotation, brightness, CLAHE, noise injection).

⚛️ Quantum Advantage

The 4-qubit VQC built with PennyLane uses RY angle-encoding + CNOT entanglement gates over 6 variational layers. The quantum state space of 2⁴ = 16 dimensions captures feature correlations missed by classical dense layers, yielding a +3.3% accuracy lift.

🚀 Innovations

Beyond classification: real-time behavior prediction via LSTM, ecosystem health scoring, IUCN Red List integration, depth zone estimation, and edge-AI optimized (TFLite + ONNX) for underwater drone deployment.
